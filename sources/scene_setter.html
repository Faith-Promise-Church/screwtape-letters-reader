The letters you have just heard came out in 1942. Almost twenty years later, in 1959, Lewis returned to Screwtape one more time. This piece is not a letter. It is a speech.

The setting moves from a private desk to a banquet hall. Screwtape is now the guest of honor at the yearly dinner of the Tempters’ Training College, and he rises to address the new graduates over their wine.

Listen for how the target changes. The letters worked on one soul at a time. The toast looks wider, at whole schools and whole societies, and at the quiet ways a culture can be steered. It is Lewis at his sharpest about the age he saw coming.

Now, the dinner is served, and Screwtape rises to speak.
